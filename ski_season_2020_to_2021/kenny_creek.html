Kenny Creek

My original goal was some west-facing trees near the bottom of Rocky Basin, but I didn't feel good about the snow conditions. Ironically, the east-facing slope I had to ski down to get into Rocky Basin, and skin up to get out, was probably more suspect. So that was probably a dumb mistake.

Fortunately, I caught Kenny Bowl (the large swath of skiable terrain north and east of Kenny Creek Cabin) in really good condition. There was a hard crust under the powder, but other than that, it was pretty amazing snow to ski. Plus, the slope angles are quite moderate in this area. It does pitch over 30 in some places, but you can avoid much of that by your choice of descent.

Though there is a great deal of skiable terrain here, it is not often skied due to its solar aspects not keeping good snow, and possibly due to the approach. Fortunately, I was able to ski down the gas pipeline, and this saved a great deal of time off my exit for the day.

It's clear from the video that I'm horribly out of shape and a horrible skier.
