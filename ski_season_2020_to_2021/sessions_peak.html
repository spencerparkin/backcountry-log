Sessions Peak

I felt like conditions were stable enough to try Session's Peak. We had had a totally green ava-rose for the last several days, and I think we were getting freeze/thaw cycles too. I should have read the UAC forecast for the day more carefully, though, because it did mention that some of the new storm snow could be a bit prone to slide on old snow in some isolated areas. I was able to confirm this with some of my skinning on the approach.

As I recall, there was a lot of bare crust and wind-slab, both hard to ski. The wind that created these conditions may have saved me, because it may have blown out the new snow to prevent it from forming a slab that could have slid on me. Typically, wind is bad, because it overloads the lee-side of slopes, creating a slab that can then release on you; but in this case, it may have saved me.
