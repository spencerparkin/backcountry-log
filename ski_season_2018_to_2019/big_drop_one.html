Big Drop One

This is the one and only time I went skiing with John-Mills--a fantastic skier. His father is in my ward, and talking with him, it was funny to hear him say that his son (John), "thinks he owns the place," referring to Bountiful Ridge. According to Owen, there was a long period of time when it was basically just him and John that frequented the area.

I don't know why John wanted to ski with me in the first place, but I'm absolutely certain I disapointed him. Since the footage was taken above, I'd like to believe my skiing has greatly improved, but as you can see from the video, my skiing was just awful.

I'm guessing that John waited somewhere between 15 to 30 minutes at his car while I was still trying to get down North Canyon road at the end of the day. I'm always in full snow-plow mode going down that road. I think he parallel skied the whole thing--something I could never do, even today with enough confidence to get down some of the double-blacks at Brighton's Milly lift. (I never look good going down those runs, but I can get down them.)

Needless to say, John was too busy to ski again the rest of the season. But it was nice of him to ski with me at least once.

As the name somewhat-implies, there are multiple "big drops" beyond Black's Peak and the Burro Mine along Bountiful Ridge. These are Ownen's names, and the area is often skied out by Wasatch Powder-Bird Guides.
