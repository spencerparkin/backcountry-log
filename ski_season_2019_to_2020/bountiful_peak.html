Bountiful Peak

The best skiing off Bountiful Peak, I've heard, is on the east side, going down the shoots. The west side is much more mellow, and that's what I skied this day.

A day or two later, John Mills, surprisingly, called me over to help him move a couch out of my neighbor's house. He asked if I'd skied anything lately, and when I told him about Bountiful Peak, he then asked if it was all wind-jacked. He was spot on. I'm not sure if that's because he's tried the same place, or if he derived the conclusion from his snow senses. I should have asked. In any case, I screwed up the social encounter, as always.

As I was skiing back down the ridge to Mid-Way Peak, I thought to gain some speed in order to ski up a short up-hill section. This proved to be a big mistake. I was going down some really hard and bumpy wind-slab which bucked me off my skis so hard that I popped out of both tech bindings! I was very lucky to not get hurt, and that I was able to find both my skis a short distance away. They could have gone sliding down the side of the ridge.

I took the Old North trail to Mid-Way peak on approach thinking it would be possible to ski down some of it. I gave up not long after leaving Mid-Way peak. It was too steep and too confined.
