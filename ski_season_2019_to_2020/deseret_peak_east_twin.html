Desert Peak East Twin

Plans fell through to go up to Deseret Peak with Owen, so I just went alone.

I knew the avalanche dragon was just going to be wet slides, which are much more manageable than slab avalanches, but of course, no less dangerous if you're caught and carried. Wet slides are more likely to break at your feet while slab avalanches will typically break will above you--giving you little hope for escape.

The snow didn't really soften up that day, because the sun never came out, except for one brief moment, and when it did, I was surprised just how powerful the heat was. If that had been aloud to continue, things would have become unstable pretty quick, so I was lucky it was overcast all day. In fact, while exiting, a very thick fog enveloped the peak, which would have made the already-tricky rock-laden descent even trickier.

As can be seen in the video, I did have one crash. I'm glad this didn't happen higher up in the couloir, because I'm not sure I could have arrested the fall. While approaching the couloir, it looks scary-steep, but once you're on it, you realize it's not that bad. I've skied much steeper at the ski resort. Too much steeper than it was, however, and I might have backed off of it, and that's okay. No one should ever feel bad about aborting an objective in the mountains. Going home is the only requirement.
